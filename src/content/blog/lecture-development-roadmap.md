# Lecture Development Roadmap: Fundamentals of Nursing

This document outlines a series of lectures for a Fundamentals of Nursing course, based on the detailed outline from the OpenStax textbook. Each lecture topic is scoped to be a manageable unit of instruction, suitable for being developed into a complete learning session using Gagné's Nine Events of Instruction.

## Unit 1: Nursing and the Healthcare Environment

### Chapter 1: Introduction to Nursing
- **Lecture 1.1: The Evolution and Definition of Nursing.**
  - Covers: The historical evolution of nursing, key historical figures, the modern definition of nursing (ANA), and the core roles of the professional nurse.
  - *Corresponds to OpenStax Sections: 1.1, 1.2*
- **Lecture 1.2: Professional Pathways and the Future of Nursing.**
  - Covers: Nursing education pathways (LPN, ADN, BSN), the importance of professional organizations, and key trends from the "Future of Nursing" report.
  - *Corresponds to OpenStax Sections: 1.3, 1.4, 1.5*

### Chapter 2: The Nursing Profession and Healthcare Delivery Systems
- **Lecture 2.1: Healthcare Delivery Systems and the Interprofessional Team.**
  - Covers: The structure of the U.S. healthcare system, different models of care (primary, secondary, tertiary), and the roles and responsibilities of the healthcare team.
  - *Corresponds to OpenStax Sections: 2.1, 2.2*
- **Lecture 2.2: Healthcare Settings, Finance, and Trends.**
  - Covers: An overview of various healthcare settings, the concept of continuity of care, major sources of healthcare funding, and emerging trends like telehealth.
  - *Corresponds to OpenStax Sections: 2.3, 2.4, 2.5*

### Chapter 3: Legal and Ethical Issues in Nursing
- **Lecture 3.1: Legal Responsibilities in Nursing.**
  - Covers: Nurse Practice Acts, standards of care, torts (negligence, malpractice), and legal aspects of delegation and documentation.
  - *Corresponds to OpenStax Sections: 3.1, 3.5*
- **Lecture 3.2: Ethical Principles and Patient Rights.**
  - Covers: Core ethical principles (autonomy, beneficence, etc.), ethical decision-making frameworks, patient rights (informed consent), and the nurse's role as an advocate.
  - *Corresponds to OpenStax Sections: 3.2, 3.3*
- **Lecture 3.3: Confidentiality and HIPAA.**
  - Covers: The importance of patient confidentiality and the key provisions of the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA).
  - *Corresponds to OpenStax Section: 3.4*

### Chapter 4: Evidence-Based Practice and Research
- **Lecture 4.1: Foundations of Evidence-Based Practice (EBP).**
  - Covers: The definition and components of EBP, the steps of the research process, and the difference between qualitative and quantitative research.
  - *Corresponds to OpenStax Sections: 4.1, 4.2*
- **Lecture 4.2: Applying Evidence to Nursing Practice.**
  - Covers: Formulating clinical questions (PICO-T), searching for and appraising evidence, and overcoming barriers to EBP implementation.
  - *Corresponds to OpenStax Sections: 4.3, 4.4*

### Chapter 5: Cultural Competence and Health Disparities
- **Lecture 5.1: Cultural Competence and Assessment.**
  - Covers: The influence of culture on health beliefs, components of a cultural assessment, and providing culturally competent, patient-centered care.
  - *Corresponds to OpenStax Sections: 5.1, 5.2, 5.4*
- **Lecture 5.2: Health Disparities and Health Equity.**
  - Covers: The definitions of health disparities and health equity and an overview of major health disparities in the U.S.
  - *Corresponds to OpenStax Section: 5.3*

### Chapter 6: Communication and Collaboration
- **Lecture 6.1: Therapeutic Communication.**
  - Covers: The communication process, verbal vs. nonverbal communication, and the use of therapeutic communication techniques.
  - *Corresponds to OpenStax Sections: 6.1, 6.2*
- **Lecture 6.2: Interprofessional Communication and Documentation.**
  - Covers: Standardized communication tools (SBAR) for team collaboration and the principles of accurate medical record documentation.
  - *Corresponds to OpenStax Sections: 6.3, 6.4*

## Unit 2: Foundations of Nursing Practice

### Chapter 7: The Nursing Process
- **Lecture 7.1: Introduction to the Nursing Process: Assessment & Diagnosis.**
  - Covers: The five steps of the nursing process, collecting subjective and objective data, and formulating a nursing diagnosis (PES format).
  - *Corresponds to OpenStax Sections: 7.1, 7.2, 7.3*
- **Lecture 7.2: The Nursing Process: Planning, Implementation, & Evaluation.**
  - Covers: Prioritizing diagnoses, setting goals, selecting interventions, implementing the care plan, and evaluating outcomes.
  - *Corresponds to OpenStax Sections: 7.4, 7.5, 7.6*

### Chapter 8: Critical Thinking and Clinical Judgment
- **Lecture 8.1: Critical Thinking and Clinical Judgment in Nursing.**
  - Covers: The definition of critical thinking, characteristics of a critical thinker, and the application of clinical judgment models (NCSBN).
  - *Corresponds to OpenStax Sections: 8.1, 8.2, 8.3*

### Chapter 9: Documentation and Informatics
- **Lecture 9.1: Principles and Legal Aspects of Documentation.**
  - Covers: The purposes of the medical record, characteristics of effective documentation, and legal best practices.
  - *Corresponds to OpenStax Sections: 9.1, 9.3*
- **Lecture 9.2: Electronic Health Records (EHR) and Nursing Informatics.**
  - Covers: The benefits and challenges of EHRs, protecting patient privacy, and the role of nursing informatics in improving care.
  - *Corresponds to OpenStax Sections: 9.2, 9.4*

### Chapter 10: Patient Education
- **Lecture 10.1: Principles of Patient Education.**
  - Covers: Basic learning principles, the three domains of learning, assessing learning needs and barriers, and developing a teaching plan with measurable objectives.
  - *Corresponds to OpenStax Sections: 10.1, 10.2, 10.3*
- **Lecture 10.2: Implementing and Evaluating Patient Teaching.**
  - Covers: Selecting appropriate teaching strategies and using methods like "teach-back" to evaluate learning and document the educational process.
  - *Corresponds to OpenStax Section: 10.4*

### Chapter 11: Health Assessment
- **Lecture 11.1: The Health History.**
  - Covers: Comprehensive vs. focused assessments and the components and techniques for taking a thorough health history.
  - *Corresponds to OpenStax Sections: 11.1, 11.2*
- **Lecture 11.2: Physical Examination Techniques.**
  - Covers: The four techniques of physical examination (inspection, palpation, percussion, auscultation) and the documentation of findings.
  - *Corresponds to OpenStax Sections: 11.3, 11.4*

### Chapter 12: Vital Signs
- **Lecture 12.1: Temperature and Pulse.**
  - Covers: Physiology, factors affecting, and assessment of temperature and pulse.
  - *Corresponds to OpenStax Sections: 12.1, 12.2*
- **Lecture 12.2: Respirations and Blood Pressure.**
  - Covers: Physiology, factors affecting, and assessment of respirations and blood pressure.
  - *Corresponds to OpenStax Sections: 12.3, 12.4*
- **Lecture 12.3: Oxygen Saturation and Pain Assessment.**
  - Covers: Principles of pulse oximetry and the assessment of pain as the "fifth vital sign."
  - *Corresponds to OpenStax Sections: 12.5, 12.6*

### Chapter 13: Infection Prevention and Control
- **Lecture 13.1: The Chain of Infection and Standard Precautions.**
  - Covers: The six components of the chain of infection and the principles of Standard Precautions, including hand hygiene.
  - *Corresponds to OpenStax Sections: 13.1, 13.2*
- **Lecture 13.2: Transmission-Based Precautions and Asepsis.**
  - Covers: Contact, Droplet, and Airborne precautions; use of PPE; and the principles of medical and surgical asepsis.
  - *Corresponds to OpenStax Sections: 13.2, 13.3*
- **Lecture 13.3: Healthcare-Associated Infections (HAIs).**
  - Covers: Common types of HAIs and evidence-based bundles for prevention.
  - *Corresponds to OpenStax Section: 13.4*

### Chapter 14: Safety and Quality Improvement
- **Lecture 14.1: Principles of Patient Safety.**
  - Covers: The culture of safety and The Joint Commission's National Patient Safety Goals.
  - *Corresponds to OpenStax Section: 14.1*
- **Lecture 14.2: Error Prevention and Quality Improvement.**
  - Covers: Types of errors, error analysis (RCA), and an introduction to Quality Improvement models (PDSA).
  - *Corresponds to OpenStax Sections: 14.2, 14.3*
- **Lecture 14.3: Managing Specific Safety Risks.**
  - Covers: Risk factors and prevention strategies for patient falls, medication errors, and pressure injuries.
  - *Corresponds to OpenStax Section: 14.4*

## Unit 3: Physiological Needs and Basic Care

### Chapter 15: Oxygenation
- **Lecture 15.1: Respiratory Anatomy, Physiology, and Assessment.**
  - Covers: The structures of the respiratory system, the mechanics of breathing, gas exchange, and how to perform a focused respiratory assessment.
  - *Corresponds to OpenStax Sections: 15.1, 15.2*
- **Lecture 15.2: Interventions for Optimal Oxygenation.**
  - Covers: Positioning, breathing techniques, oxygen therapy devices, and basic airway management including suctioning.
  - *Corresponds to OpenStax Sections: 15.3, 15.4, 15.5*

### Chapter 16: Nutrition
- **Lecture 16.1: Nutritional Principles and Assessment.**
  - Covers: Essential macro and micronutrients, dietary guidelines, and the components of a comprehensive nutritional assessment.
  - *Corresponds to OpenStax Sections: 16.1, 16.2*
- **Lecture 16.2: Nutritional Interventions and Support.**
  - Covers: Managing malnutrition and dysphagia, therapeutic diets, and the nursing responsibilities for enteral and parenteral nutrition.
  - *Corresponds to OpenStax Sections: 16.3, 16.4*

### Chapter 17: Elimination
- **Lecture 17.1: Urinary Elimination.**
  - Covers: Anatomy and physiology of the urinary system, factors influencing urination, common alterations, and nursing interventions including catheterization.
  - *Corresponds to OpenStax Section: 17.1*
- **Lecture 17.2: Bowel Elimination.**
  - Covers: Anatomy and physiology of the GI system, factors influencing bowel elimination, common alterations, and nursing interventions including enemas and ostomy care.
  - *Corresponds to OpenStax Section: 17.2*

### Chapter 18: Fluid, Electrolyte, and Acid-Base Balance
- **Lecture 18.1: Principles of Fluid and Electrolyte Balance.**
  - Covers: Fluid compartments, movement of fluids, and the function of major electrolytes.
  - *Corresponds to OpenStax Section: 18.1*
- **Lecture 18.2: Fluid and Electrolyte Imbalances.**
  - Covers: Assessing and managing fluid volume deficit/excess and common electrolyte imbalances (Sodium, Potassium, Calcium, Magnesium).
  - *Corresponds to OpenStax Sections: 18.2, 18.3*
- **Lecture 18.3: Acid-Base Balance and IV Therapy.**
  - Covers: pH regulation, interpretation of basic ABGs, and the management of intravenous therapy.
  - *Corresponds to OpenStax Sections: 18.4, 18.5*

### Chapter 19: Mobility and Immobility
- **Lecture 19.1: Principles and Assessment of Mobility.**
  - Covers: Proper body mechanics, benefits of exercise, and how to assess a patient's mobility and musculoskeletal system.
  - *Corresponds to OpenStax Sections: 19.1, 19.2*
- **Lecture 19.2: Complications of Immobility and Nursing Interventions.**
  - Covers: The multisystem effects of immobility and interventions to prevent complications, including positioning, ROM exercises, and safe patient handling.
  - *Corresponds to OpenStax Sections: 19.3, 19.4*

### Chapter 20: Comfort, Rest, and Sleep
- **Lecture 20.1: Physiology of Sleep and Influencing Factors.**
  - Covers: The stages of sleep, circadian rhythms, and factors that affect sleep across the lifespan.
  - *Corresponds to OpenStax Sections: 20.2, 20.3*
- **Lecture 20.2: Sleep Disorders and Promoting Rest.**
  - Covers: Common sleep disorders (insomnia, sleep apnea) and nursing interventions to promote a restful environment and good sleep hygiene.
  - *Corresponds to OpenStax Sections: 20.1, 20.4, 20.5*

### Chapter 21: Pain Management
- **Lecture 21.1: The Nature and Assessment of Pain.**
  - Covers: The physiology of pain, types of pain (acute, chronic, nociceptive, neuropathic), and how to conduct a comprehensive pain assessment.
  - *Corresponds to OpenStax Sections: 21.1, 21.2*
- **Lecture 21.2: Pharmacologic and Nonpharmacologic Pain Interventions.**
  - Covers: The WHO analgesic ladder, opioids, non-opioids, and various nonpharmacologic strategies (cognitive-behavioral and physical).
  - *Corresponds to OpenStax Sections: 21.3, 21.4*

### Chapter 22: Hygiene
- **Lecture 22.1: Principles of Hygiene and Personal Care.**
  - Covers: The importance of hygiene, factors influencing practices, and procedures for bathing, perineal care, and oral hygiene.
  - *Corresponds to OpenStax Sections: 22.1, 22.2, 22.3*
- **Lecture 22.2: Assisting with Hygiene.**
  - Covers: Procedures for hair, nail, foot, eye, and ear care, including care of dentures and hearing aids.
  - *Corresponds to OpenStax Sections: 22.4, 22.5*

### Chapter 23: Skin Integrity and Wound Care
- **Lecture 23.1: Skin Integrity and Pressure Injury Prevention.**
  - Covers: Skin anatomy, factors affecting skin integrity, risk assessment (Braden Scale), and pressure injury staging and prevention.
  - *Corresponds to OpenStax Sections: 23.1, 23.2*
- **Lecture 23.2: Wound Healing and Management.**
  - Covers: The phases of wound healing, complications, and the principles of wound management, including assessment, cleansing, and dressing selection.
  - *Corresponds to OpenStax Sections: 23.3, 23.4*

## Unit 4: Medication Administration and Management

### Chapter 24: Principles of Medication Administration
- **Lecture 24.1: Legal, Ethical, and Safety Principles of Medication Administration.**
  - Covers: Legal foundations, medication orders, the "rights" of medication administration, and the three checks process.
  - *Corresponds to OpenStax Sections: 24.1, 24.2, 24.3, 24.4*

### Chapter 25: Dosage Calculation and Preparation
- **Lecture 25.1: Dosage Calculation.**
  - Covers: Systems of measurement and the use of various methods (ratio-proportion, formula, dimensional analysis) for oral, parenteral, and IV calculations.
  - *Corresponds to OpenStax Sections: 25.1, 25.2, 25.3, 25.4, 25.5*
- **Lecture 25.2: Safe Medication Preparation.**
  - Covers: The safe preparation of oral medications and injections from vials and ampules.
  - *Corresponds to OpenStax Section: 25.6*

### Chapter 26: Routes of Medication Administration
- **Lecture 26.1: Oral and Topical Medication Administration.**
  - Covers: Procedures for administering oral, sublingual, buccal, topical, and transdermal medications.
  - *Corresponds to OpenStax Sections: 26.1, 26.3*
- **Lecture 26.2: Parenteral Medication Administration.**
  - Covers: Techniques for administering intradermal, subcutaneous, and intramuscular injections, including site selection and the Z-track method.
  - *Corresponds to OpenStax Section: 26.2*
- **Lecture 26.3: Mucous Membrane and Inhaled Medication Administration.**
  - Covers: Procedures for administering medications via the ophthalmic, otic, nasal, vaginal, rectal, and inhalation routes.
  - *Corresponds to OpenStax Sections: 26.4, 26.5*

### Chapter 27: Medication Safety and Error Prevention
- **Lecture 27.1: Medication Error Prevention and Response.**
  - Covers: Types and causes of medication errors, the role of technology, strategies for high-alert medications, and how to respond to and report an error.
  - *Corresponds to OpenStax Sections: 27.1, 27.2, 27.3*
- **Lecture 27.2: Medication Reconciliation and Patient Education.**
  - Covers: The process of medication reconciliation during transitions in care and the nurse's role in patient education for medication safety.
  - *Corresponds to OpenStax Sections: 27.4, 27.5*

## Unit 5: Perioperative and Procedural Nursing

### Chapter 28: Perioperative Nursing
- **Lecture 28.1: The Preoperative Phase.**
  - Covers: The phases of surgery, preoperative patient assessment, informed consent, and preoperative teaching.
  - *Corresponds to OpenStax Sections: 28.1, 28.2*
- **Lecture 28.2: The Intraoperative and Postoperative Phases.**
  - Covers: The surgical team, anesthesia, nursing roles in the OR, and immediate care of the patient in the PACU.
  - *Corresponds to OpenStax Sections: 28.3, 28.4*

### Chapter 29: Procedural Care and Skills
- **Lecture 29.1: Sterile Technique and Specimen Collection.**
  - Covers: The principles of surgical asepsis, applying sterile gloves, and procedures for collecting various laboratory specimens.
  - *Corresponds to OpenStax Sections: 29.1, 29.2*
- **Lecture 29.2: IV Therapy and Urinary Catheterization Skills.**
  - Covers: Initiating and maintaining peripheral IVs and the insertion and care of urinary catheters.
  - *Corresponds to OpenStax Sections: 29.3, 29.4*
- **Lecture 29.3: Nasogastric (NG) Tube Management.**
  - Covers: Insertion, placement verification, and maintenance of NG tubes for gastric decompression.
  - *Corresponds to OpenStax Section: 29.5*

## Unit 6: Psychosocial and Spiritual Needs

### Chapter 30: Psychosocial Assessment and Support
- **Lecture 30.1: Self-Concept and Sexuality.**
  - Covers: The components of self-concept and the nurse's role in addressing sexual health.
  - *Corresponds to OpenStax Sections: 30.1, 30.2*
- **Lecture 30.2: Stress, Coping, Loss, and Grief.**
  - Covers: The stress response, coping mechanisms, and the grieving process.
  - *Corresponds to OpenStax Sections: 30.3, 30.4, 30.5*

### Chapter 31: Spirituality and End-of-Life Care
- **Lecture 31.1: Spiritual Assessment and Care.**
  - Covers: The concept of spirituality, spiritual assessment, and interventions to support patients' spiritual needs.
  - *Corresponds to OpenStax Sections: 31.1, 31.2, 31.3*
- **Lecture 31.2: Principles of End-of-Life Care.**
  - Covers: Palliative vs. hospice care, legal/ethical issues (advance directives), and nursing care of the dying patient.
  - *Corresponds to OpenStax Sections: 31.4, 31.5*

### Chapter 32: Grief, Loss, and Palliative Care
- **Lecture 32.1: Grief, Loss, and Palliative Care.**
  - Covers: Theories of grief, the principles of palliative and hospice care, and nursing interventions to support families.
  - *Corresponds to OpenStax Sections: 32.1, 32.2, 32.3, 32.4*

### Chapter 33: Stress and Coping
- **Lecture 33.1: Stress, Coping, and Self-Care.**
  - Covers: The nature of stress, assessment of stress and coping, stress management interventions, and the importance of self-care for the nurse.
  - *Corresponds to OpenStax Sections: 33.1, 33.2, 33.3, 33.4*

### Chapter 34: Mental Health Nursing Basics
- **Lecture 34.1: Foundations of Mental Health Nursing.**
  - Covers: The therapeutic nurse-patient relationship, conducting a mental status exam, and ensuring patient safety.
  - *Corresponds to OpenStax Sections: 34.1, 34.2, 34.3, 34.5*
- **Lecture 34.2: Overview of Common Mental Health Disorders.**
  - Covers: Recognizing the signs and symptoms of common anxiety, depressive, bipolar, and psychotic disorders.
  - *Corresponds to OpenStax Section: 34.4*

## Unit 7: Professionalism, Leadership, and Growth

### Chapter 35: Professionalism and Leadership
- **Lecture 35.1: Professionalism, Leadership, and Advocacy.**

  - *Corresponds to OpenStax Sections: 35.1, 35.2, 35.3, 35.4*

### Chapter 36: Time Management and Prioritization
- **Lecture 36.1: Time Management, Prioritization, and Delegation.**

  - *Corresponds to OpenStax Sections: 36.1, 36.2, 36.3*

### Chapter 37: Delegation and Supervision
- **Lecture 37.1: Delegation and Supervision in Practice.**
  - Covers: A deeper dive into the five rights of delegation, the RN's accountability, and principles of supervision.
  - *Corresponds to OpenStax Sections: 37.1, 37.2, 37.3, 37.4*

### Chapter 38: Quality Improvement and Patient Safety
- **Lecture 38.1: Quality Improvement and Patient Safety.**
  - Covers: The domains of healthcare quality, QI models (PDSA), the role of EBP, and the nurse's role in maintaining a culture of safety.
  - *Corresponds to OpenStax Sections: 38.1, 38.2, 38.3, 38.4*

### Chapter 39: Lifelong Learning and Career Development
- **Lecture 39.1: Lifelong Learning and Career Development.**
  - Covers: The importance of continuing education, developing a professional portfolio, career planning, and the benefits of professional engagement.
  - *Corresponds to OpenStax Sections: 39.1, 39.2, 39.3, 39.4*

### Chapter 40: Nursing in the Community
- **Lecture 40.1: Community and Public Health Nursing.**
  - Covers: The principles of community health, the core functions of public health, and the three levels of prevention.
  - *Corresponds to OpenStax Sections: 40.1, 40.2*
- **Lecture 40.2: Roles and Settings for Community Nurses.**
  - Covers: The varied roles of nurses in public health, home health, school, and occupational settings, and the process of community assessment.
  - *Corresponds to OpenStax Sections: 40.3, 40.4*

### Chapter 41: Disaster Preparedness and Emergency Response
- **Lecture 41.1: Disaster Preparedness and Response.**
  - Covers: The disaster management cycle, personal and community preparedness, and the principles of mass casualty triage (START).
  - *Corresponds to OpenStax Sections: 41.1, 41.2, 41.3*
- **Lecture 41.2: Specific Emergencies and Psychological Aspects.**
  - Covers: Nursing considerations for bioterrorism, chemical incidents, and the principles of psychological first aid.
  - *Corresponds to OpenStax Sections: 41.4, 41.5*

### Chapter 42: Global Health and Nursing
- **Lecture 42.1: Global Health and Nursing.**
  - Covers: Key global health issues, the role of major health organizations (WHO), the UN's SDGs, and the nurse's role in global health.
  - *Corresponds to OpenStax Sections: 42.1, 42.2, 42.3, 42.4*

### Chapter 43: Trends and Future Directions in Nursing
- **Lecture 43.1: Trends and Future Directions in Nursing.**
  - Covers: The impact of changing demographics, the shift to population health, the influence of technology, and the expanding roles for nurses as leaders.
  - *Corresponds to OpenStax Sections: 43.1, 43.2, 43.3, 43.4*
